# Lebanon vs MENA Region: Inflation Crisis Analysis

## Business Question
How does Lebanon's post-2019 inflation crisis compare to other MENA region economies?

## Data Source
World Bank Open Data — Inflation, consumer prices (annual %), 2015-2024.
Countries: Lebanon, Jordan, Egypt, Tunisia, Morocco, Turkiye, Iraq.

## Key Findings
Lebanon's inflation peaked at 221% in 2023 — far higher than every other country in the comparison, including Turkiye (72% peak), while the rest of the region stayed under 20% throughout. The crisis, rooted in the 2019 economic collapse and worsened by COVID-19, severely eroded purchasing power and pushed much of the middle class into poverty.

## Limitations
This analysis looks at inflation alone. Lebanon's crisis also involved a banking sector freeze on withdrawals and a sharp GDP contraction — factors worth examining in a follow-up analysis.
